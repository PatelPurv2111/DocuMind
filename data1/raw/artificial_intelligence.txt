Artificial intelligence (AI) is a broad field of computer science focused on building machines capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include reasoning, learning from experience, recognizing patterns, understanding language, and making decisions. AI systems attempt to simulate aspects of human cognition through algorithms, data processing, and computational models. Over the past decade, advances in computing power and the availability of massive datasets have significantly accelerated progress in artificial intelligence research.

AI technologies are used across many industries. For example, recommendation systems on streaming platforms analyze user behavior to suggest movies or music. In healthcare, AI assists doctors by analyzing medical images and identifying potential abnormalities. Financial institutions use AI to detect fraudulent transactions and assess credit risk. Autonomous vehicles rely heavily on AI systems that process data from cameras and sensors to understand the environment and make driving decisions.

Artificial intelligence can be categorized into different levels. Narrow AI refers to systems designed for specific tasks such as image recognition or language translation. These systems perform well within a limited domain but cannot generalize beyond their designed capabilities. General AI, sometimes referred to as strong AI, would have the ability to understand and perform any intellectual task that a human can do. However, general AI remains largely theoretical and has not yet been achieved.

Ethical considerations are an important aspect of AI development. Algorithms trained on biased data can produce unfair or discriminatory outcomes. For instance, facial recognition systems have sometimes shown lower accuracy for certain demographic groups. As a result, researchers and organizations are increasingly focusing on fairness, transparency, and accountability when building AI systems.

Artificial intelligence continues to evolve rapidly and is expected to transform many aspects of society. From intelligent assistants and smart homes to advanced robotics and medical diagnostics, AI is shaping the future of technology and human interaction with machines.
