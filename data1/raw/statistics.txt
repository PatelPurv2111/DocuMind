Statistics is a branch of mathematics concerned with collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. It provides the theoretical foundation for many data analysis and machine learning techniques. Statistical methods help researchers draw meaningful conclusions from data and quantify uncertainty in their findings.

There are two main branches of statistics: descriptive statistics and inferential statistics. Descriptive statistics summarize and describe the characteristics of a dataset. Common measures include the mean, median, mode, variance, and standard deviation. These metrics provide insights into the distribution and variability of data.

Inferential statistics involves making predictions or generalizations about a population based on a sample of data. Techniques such as hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis allow researchers to determine whether observed patterns are statistically significant.

Statistics plays a crucial role in many fields, including economics, medicine, social sciences, and engineering. In machine learning, statistical concepts help evaluate model performance and ensure that predictions are reliable.

Understanding statistical principles is essential for interpreting data correctly and avoiding misleading conclusions.
