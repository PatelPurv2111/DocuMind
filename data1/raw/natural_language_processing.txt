Natural language processing (NLP) is a field of artificial intelligence that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. Human language is complex and filled with ambiguity, making it difficult for machines to process. NLP combines linguistics, machine learning, and statistical methods to analyze text and speech data.

One of the first steps in NLP is text preprocessing. This involves tasks such as tokenization, which splits text into individual words or tokens, and stop-word removal, which eliminates common words that may not add meaningful information. Stemming and lemmatization are techniques used to reduce words to their root forms.

NLP systems perform a wide range of tasks. Sentiment analysis determines whether a piece of text expresses positive, negative, or neutral emotions. Named entity recognition identifies important entities such as people, organizations, and locations in text. Machine translation converts text from one language to another, while question-answering systems retrieve relevant information to respond to user queries.

Recent advances in deep learning and transformer architectures have significantly improved the capabilities of NLP systems. These models are trained on large amounts of text data and can generate coherent paragraphs, summarize documents, and even hold conversations with users.

NLP plays an important role in many everyday technologies, including virtual assistants, chatbots, search engines, and automated customer support systems.
