Machine learning operations, commonly known as MLOps, is a set of practices that combines machine learning, software engineering, and DevOps principles to manage the lifecycle of machine learning systems. The goal of MLOps is to streamline the development, deployment, and maintenance of machine learning models in production environments.

Traditional machine learning workflows often involve experimentation in research environments. However, deploying models into production requires additional processes such as version control, monitoring, and automation. MLOps addresses these challenges by introducing standardized pipelines for data preparation, model training, testing, and deployment.

Monitoring is a critical aspect of MLOps. Once a model is deployed, its performance must be tracked over time. Changes in data distribution can lead to performance degradation, a phenomenon known as data drift. Monitoring systems detect such changes and trigger alerts when necessary.

By adopting MLOps practices, organizations can ensure that machine learning models remain reliable, scalable, and maintainable throughout their lifecycle.
